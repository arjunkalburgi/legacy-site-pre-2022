ShoutBox Redesigned

After nearly a year, my partner Harry and I decided that ShoutBox needed a few touch ups in the branding and hosting side of things before being ready to be marketed to the world. I took on the branding by giving the website and the extension a new look and feel, and updating the Chrome extension to match.

The design for the site was borrowed from this html5up template, and modified to give the desired look - matching the new colours we chose. This was done over the course of a week after exams were completed by updating the ejs to the template and making sure it could read the new css file folder. The pictures on the site and all the logos were designed by moi, I hope you notice the Beyonce Lemonade easter egg in the example pictures on the website!

RDF for the Dean's Research Award

The University of Alberta has a program for students called the "Dean's Research Award", upon signing up for the the program, the student conducts some type of research with the help/guidance of a professor that agrees. Dr. Reformat, from my RDF work in the summer, agreed to work with me on a new project that would continue on the work from the summer - an RDF Crawler.

The Crawler crawls the DBpedia database for RDF tripes containing the properties that you specify and outputs the result into a file. A presentation was required in order to complete and receive the award.

Both code for the Crawler, and the presentation are linked on the card.

Fire Escape

For my second HackED hackathon, I tried to go bigger than ever. Knowing that hardware hacks that are rooted in reality usually do win hackathons, I assembled a team that would be able to design and build an arcade game with an element of gamifying education. As a team we came up with the idea that a puzzle game where the user places fire safety items where the AI people can find them to save themselves when the fire starts until the fire truck can get there to save them.

The game was built with SDL C++, which is much more low level than I'm used to. I was tasked with the UI of the game and had a lot of work trying to create something that looked nice with a minimum amount of tools.

Fire Escape was made to be an arcade game and an arcade box and hardware controls were built for it for the hackathon. However the software was buggy and our presentation did not appeal to the judges so we didn't place. Fire Escape still has to be worked on, and the team and I plan on working on it throughout Summer '16.
