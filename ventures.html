Revitalizing ENCMP

Winter 2017
At the Faculty of Engineering in the UofA, programming doesn't get the same type of interest as it does globally. A big part of the reason behind this is that the first year programming course tends to scare people away. Brittany and I are working with Ivan Fair and Dileepan Joseph to change this. Dr. Joseph, the main prof of ENCMP, especially is making great strides and I'm very happy to be involved in tooling him with what students want to see!

HackTime

Winter 2017
HackTime seeks to bring about a hackathon-like experience for coders around the University. The goal is to encourage students to work on side projects by creating a safe space and community. This initiative begins in the Fall of 2017.

Making events bigger and better might be more difficult than doing them the first time. This year we sported more hackers, more sponsors, more mentors, more judges and we still accomplished the goal of inspiring and growing passion. It reminds me why I do it all.

CE approval of AU Courses

Fall 2016
Disappointed in my options for electives, I took to the list of approved Athabasca University courses for credit. Unfortunately, neither of these interested me either. Out of interest, I looked through AU courses offered for ones I would enroll in and upon finding two, decided to approach the Director of Computer Engineering about them. Understanding that I brought him courses the University did not offer already, he approved them for me and added them to the list of approved courses from AU. :D I was able to expand the scope of my degree for myself and for others.
